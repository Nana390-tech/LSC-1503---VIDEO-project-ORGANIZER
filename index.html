Introduction

Introduce yourself & set the scene

⬜ ▼
📌 What to include
  • Greet your audience warmly
  • State your full name and student ID number
  • Hold up your ID card to the camera for verification
  • Give a short overview of what your video will cover (all 5 parts)
  • Keep it engaging — a strong opening hooks your audience!
Try starting with: "Hello, my name is [Name], my student ID is [ID], and today I am going to take you through my Communication Skills Portfolio…" Then briefly name each of the 5 parts you will discuss. Smile and speak clearly — first impressions count!

LPA Reflection

Reflect on one Learning Portfolio Artifact

⬜ ▼
📌 What to include
  • Name the specific LPA activity you chose
  • Describe what the activity was — what did you do?
  • Explain why you chose this LPA — what made it meaningful?
  • Share what you learned from it — skills, insights, growth
  • Be honest and personal — this is YOUR reflection
Structure it as: "For my LPA reflection, I chose [name of LPA]. This activity involved [brief description]. I chose it because [reason]. Through this activity, I learned [what you learned]." Try to connect your learning to real-life communication skills.

Study Skill Reflection

Share a study skill you developed

⬜ ▼
📌 What to include
  • Name the specific study skill you are discussing
  • Explain what the skill is — define it briefly for the audience
  • Tell us why you chose this skill — why was it important to you?
  • Reflect on what you learned and how you plan to use it
  • Give a specific example of how you applied this skill
Possible study skills: note-taking strategies, time management, active listening, mind mapping, peer feedback, vocabulary building, etc. Try: "One study skill I developed this semester is [skill]. This means [definition]. I chose to focus on this because [reason]. I used it when [example], and I learned that [insight]."

Area for Improvement

What / So What / Now What reflection

⬜ ▼
📌 What to include — The What / So What / Now What Framework
  • WHAT: Identify the area you want to improve — be specific and honest
  • SO WHAT: Why does this matter? What impact does this weakness have?
  • NOW WHAT: What is your plan to improve? What steps will you take?
  • This shows self-awareness and growth mindset — highly valued in communication!
Be honest but professional. Example: "An area I want to improve is my confidence when speaking in front of large groups (WHAT). This matters because strong public speaking is essential in my future career (SO WHAT). To improve, I plan to join speaking clubs, practise with friends, and record myself regularly (NOW WHAT)."

Conclusion

Summarise & close powerfully

⬜ ▼
📌 What to include
  • Briefly summarise all 5 parts of your video (1–2 sentences each)
  • End with a general closing statement — something meaningful, inspiring, or reflective
  • Thank your audience for watching — be warm and genuine
  • Your conclusion should feel like a satisfying, confident ending
Try: "In this video, I introduced myself, reflected on my LPA, discussed a study skill, identified an area for improvement, and I hope this portfolio demonstrates my growth as a communicator. Learning to communicate effectively is not just an academic skill — it is a life skill. Thank you so much for watching."
